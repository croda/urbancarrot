Do you want to be a part of the green change in your city?

What?

Urban heat island is a phenomenon that refers to urban and suburban areas which have a higher temperature than their surroundings. As cities are growing, more green surface is being replaced by concrete buildings (and asphalt roads).

Our goal is to inform the community and engage it in the process of re-greening our environment.

Anthropogenic Heat

Heat produced by human activates by totaling the energy for heating, cooling, running appliances, transport etc.

Proeperties of Urban Materials

The emission, reflection, heat capacity and absorption of sun’s energy from surface materials on buildings.

Urban Geometry

- The dimensions and spacing of buildings within a city that influence wind flow, energy absorption and a surface radiation.

Consequences

Energy Consumption

High energy demands for cooling and adds pressure on electricity grid during period of demand.

Air Quality

Increasing the energy demands lead to higher levels of air pollution and GHG emissions, also warmer air accelerates chemical reactions that produce ground-level ozone, or smog.

Human Health & Comfort

which can cause breathing problems, heat cramps, and heat stroke, and may lead to illness or even death in different age groups.

Water Quality

Water temperature affects all aspects of aquatic life, especially the metabolism and reproduction of many aquatic species.

Solutions

Trees and Vegetation

Provide shade and cool the air through evapotranspiration.

Learn More
Cool Roofs

Reflect or release the sun’s solar energy rather than retain it, are typically 28°–33° C cooler than traditional roofs.

Learn More
Green Roofs

Replace heat-absorbing traditional roofing materials surface with plants, shrubs, and small trees.

Learn More
Cool Pavements

Store less heat and reflect more solar radiation than conventional pavements.
